EMV Bank Mobile App case study

Group UX/UI Research and Design Project

With Millennials being the largest group of consumers currently, every traditional business must adapt to their ways in order to survive and grow.

Banks are no exception.

A group of students enrolled in a UX/UI design bootcamp decided to take on the challenge of studying the financial habits of Millennials and present recommendations to a local community bank to increase their mobile presence.

In a 4-weeks long project, three students conducted research and designed a mobile app as a solution - the EMV Bank Mobile app.

My role was that of Lead UX Researcher and Designer.

Research

Secondary Research, Interviews and Surveys

Who are Millennials? Millennials are defined as young men and women, in the age group of 24-39 years in 2020
(The Pew Research Center).

We conducted personal interviews, a survey and secondary research to discover potential problems Millennials might be facing in their current digital financial-management practices.

Secondary Research Results:
1) Millennials lack financial education and face financial insecurity.

2) They use multiple financial service providers and switch based upon perceived value and perks.

Primary Research Results:
1) Difficult to consolidate and operate multiple accounts at various financial institutions. 2) Lack of financial education leading to financial security.

Problem and Solution

"How Might We Provide Meaningful Financial Services to Millennials?""

1) Simplified Financial Account Management

2) Personalized Education Inculcating Financial Security

We believe that if EMV bank were to address these needs of Millennials through their mobile app, it could potentially increase its customer base significantly to ensure its future growth.



Testing/Iteration

Rapid mock-up testing and iterations with 10 users

The concept of the app was interesting to Millennial users

Gains:
1) Consolidation of multiple institutions' accounts on one platform

2) Mobile banking app would support financial security through education

3) Eye scan technology intriguing

Pains:
1) Missing or unclear coaching screens

2) Gamification of education attractive to younger users, seemed gimmicky to older.
